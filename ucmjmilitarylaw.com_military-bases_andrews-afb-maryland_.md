## Andrews Air Force Base, Maryland \| Military Legal Guide

**Andrews Air Force Base‚Äînow Joint Base Andrews**‚Äîis home to Air Force One and key support missions for national-level government operations. ## Understanding the UCMJ at Andrews

All service members at Andrews are governed by the UCMJ regardless of duty status or rank. Infractions‚Äîwhether on base or off‚Äîcan have serious consequences.

- **Article 92 ‚Äì Failure to Obey Orders**
- **Article 120 ‚Äì Sexual Misconduct or Assault**
- **Article 107 ‚Äì False Official Statements**
- **Article 128b ‚Äì Domestic Violence**
- **Article 134 ‚Äì Conduct Unbecoming / General Misconduct**

## Administrative Actions You Might Face

- **Article 15 (NJP):** Commanders may impose punishment but you can refuse it in favor of a court-martial.
- **GOMOR:** A General Officer Memorandum of Reprimand may be placed in both local and permanent records.
- **Administrative Separation:** May threaten your benefits and future service.
- **Command-Directed Investigation:** Early-stage inquiry that often leads to further action.

## Legal Assistance at Andrews AFB

The base legal office provides routine assistance‚Äîwills, POCs, basic NJP counseling‚Äîbut does not represent members in contested proceedings such as courts-martial or separation boards. Independent counsel may be needed for serious cases.

## Common Legal Issues for Service Members at Andrews AFB, Maryland

### Court-Martial Under the UCMJ

Soldiers at Andrews AFB, Maryland face charges under:

- **Article 120** ‚Äì Sexual Assault
- **Article 128b** ‚Äì Domestic Violence
- **Article 92** ‚Äì Failure to Obey
- **Article 133/134** ‚Äì Misconduct

Do not talk to CID or command without a civilian defense lawyer. Your words will be used against you.

### Article 15 / NJP

Article 15s are often offered for minor misconduct but can derail your career. You may have the option to refuse NJP and request a court-martial. Make this decision carefully‚Äîwith legal counsel.

### GOMORs

A General Officer Memorandum of Reprimand can be filed locally or permanently. A permanent GOMOR can destroy promotion chances and trigger separation. A well-written rebuttal may save your file‚Äîand your future.

### Administrative Separation & Boards

Enlisted soldiers may face a **Chapter Separation** while officers face a **Board of Inquiry (BOI)**. These boards determine your discharge characterization and your access to VA benefits, retirement, and more.

### Command-Directed Investigations (CDI)

Often triggered by SHARP/SAPR complaints, CDIs are administrative‚Äîbut serious. You have the right to remain silent and the right to hire a civilian lawyer to protect your interests during and after the investigation.
